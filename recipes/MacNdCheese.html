Mac and Cheese

Description

Macaroni and cheese, often abbreviated as mac and cheese, is a classic food that originated in italy but has become a staple in American Cuisine. It consist of tender macaroni pasta enveloped in a rich, creamy cheese sauce. The dish is typically made by meltin cheddar cheese into a bechamel sauce. Mac and Cheese can be baked for a crispy topping or served straight from the stovetop for a gooey, indulgent treat. It is beloved for its simplicity, versatality and nostalogic appeal.

Ingredients

  • Macaroni Pasta
  • Butter
  • All-Purpose Flour
  • Milk
  • Cheedar Cheese
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Steps

  1. Cook The pasta. Boil, Drain and set aside
  2. Make the Roux: melt the butter over medium heat, once melted add flour and stir constatnly
  3. Add The milk: Gradually add the milk to the roux, while whisking it continously to prevent lumps.
  4. Add the cheese: Remove the saucepan from heat and stir in the shredded chedder cheese until it is completely melted.
  5. Combine: Add the cooked pasta to the cheese sauce and stir until pasta is coated
  6. Serve: For a baked version transfer to baking dish, top with extra cheese, and bake at 350 F for 20 minutes
